Web 3.0 and the Future of the Decentralized "Link in Bio"

Published on January 28, 2026 • 6 min read

The current internet is rented. Web 3.0 is owned. How does this affect the humble bio link?

ENS Domains

`yourname.eth` is not just a wallet; it's a website. Tools are emerging that allow you to host your entire bio link profile directly on IPFS (InterPlanetary File System), meaning it can never be taken down by a centralized server.

Wallet as Identity

In the future, "following" someone might mean connecting your wallet to theirs. This enables direct micropayments without intermediaries (like Patreon or YouTube taking a cut).

NFT Galleries

For digital artists, the bio link is becoming a gallery. Embedding your OpenSea or Foundation collection directly allows users to bid on your work without leaving your profile.

Conclusion

We aren't there yet. But the shift from "Platform Centric" to "User Centric" identity is inevitable. Vontage's serverless architecture is a step in that direction—YOU hold the data (in the URL), not us.
