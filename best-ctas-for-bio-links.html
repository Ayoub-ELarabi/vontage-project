The Best Call-to-Actions (CTAs) for Higher Click-Through Rates

Published on January 28, 2026 • 4 min read

You can have the best content in the world, but if your button says "Link 1", nobody is clicking it. The text on your button—the Call to Action (CTA)—is the final nudge that convinces a user to act.

Active vs. Passive Voice

Passive: "New Video Available"
Active: "Watch the New Video 🎬"
Always use active verbs. "Get", "Watch", "Read", "Join", "Claim". These words demand action.

Benefit-Driven CTAs

Don't tell them what it is; tell them what they get. Instead of "Newsletter Signup", try "Get Smarter in 5 min/week". Instead of "My Fitness Guide", try "Transform your Body". Focus on the result, not the vehicle.

Create Urgency

Fear Of Missing Out (FOMO) is real. "Limited Slots Open", "Sale Ends Tonight", or "Join 5,000+ Others" creates a psychological need to act now rather than later.

Emoji Power

On mobile, emojis catch the eye. A "👉" points the way. A "🔥" signals something hot/new. Use them to break up the text, but don't overdo it. One emoji per button is the sweet spot.

Keep it Short

Button text should be scannable. 2-5 words max. If it spills onto two lines, it looks messy and reduces readability. Keep it punchy.
