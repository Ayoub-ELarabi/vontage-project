Bio Link vs. Personal Website: Which Do You Need?

Published on January 28, 2026 • 7 min read

For decades, the "Personal Website" was the gold standard of digital identity. But in the era of TikTok, Instagram, and fast content, the traditional website is becoming surprisingly obsolete for individual creators. Here is why.

The Cost factor

A WordPress site costs money. You pay for hosting ($10/mo), a domain ($15/yr), and potentially premium themes ($50+). Vontage costs $0. Because it uses URL-based data persistence, you don't even pay for a database. For a creator just starting out, keeping overheads at zero is crucial.

Maintenance Nightmares

Websites break. Plugins need updating. Security patches need applying. A bio link page is static. It just works. It exists to do one thing: route traffic. It doesn't need a contact form plugin or a complex SEO sitemap.

Mobile-First Navigation

Traditional websites are often designed for desktop and "responsive" for mobile. Bio links are **native** to mobile. They feature big, tappable buttons that are thumb-friendly. When 90% of your traffic comes from a phone (Instagram/TikTok), optimizing for that experience is non-negotiable.

When do you need a website?

If you are selling complex enterprise SaaS, creating a custom web-app, or running a massive e-commerce store with 1000+ SKUs, you need a website. If you are a creator, coach, or influencer selling a few digital products or directing people to YouTube videos, a Bio Link is superior.

Conclusion

Don't overcomplicate your digital presence. Start with a Vontage link. It takes 30 seconds to set up, costs nothing, and converts better on mobile than a $5,000 custom website.
